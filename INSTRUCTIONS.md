# Chalk — Beta Tester Installation Guide

**Requirements:** Google Chrome on desktop (Windows, Mac, or Linux)

---

## Step 1 — Get the file

Download `chalk-extension-0.1.0-chrome.zip` from the link George sent you.

## Step 2 — Unzip

Right-click the zip file and extract it. You'll get a folder called `chalk-extension-0.0.1-chrome`. Put it somewhere permanent — your Documents folder works well. Chrome needs the folder to stay in place, so don't delete it after installing.

## Step 3 — Open Chrome extensions

Type `chrome://extensions` into your address bar and press Enter.

## Step 4 — Enable Developer Mode

Toggle **Developer mode** on using the switch in the top-right corner of the page.

## Step 5 — Load the extension

Click **Load unpacked**, then select the `chalk-extension-0.0.1-chrome` folder from Step 2.

Chalk will appear in your extensions list. To keep it handy, pin it to your toolbar: click the puzzle piece icon in the top-right of Chrome, find Chalk, and click the pin.

## Step 6 — Start drawing

1. Go to any webpage
2. Click the **Chalk icon** in your toolbar — the overlay activates (badge shows **ON**)
3. A drawing toolbar appears on the right — pick a color, brush size, and start drawing
4. Click the icon again (or the **x** button in the toolbar) to hide the overlay
5. Reload the page — your drawings come back, and you'll see anything others have drawn on the same URL

---

## Notes

- Chrome will show a banner saying *"You have extensions in developer mode"* — that's expected and safe to dismiss.
- Chalk is Chrome-only for now.
- Annotations are shared — anyone with Chalk installed will see what you draw on the same page.
